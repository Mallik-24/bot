      According to the Question, it is given that:

      • Frame Size: 5 million bits = 5 * 106 bits
      • Length of the link = 2000 Km = 2000 * 103 m
      • Speed of the light inside the link = 2 * 108 m/s
      • Link Bandwidth = 5 Mbps = 5 * 106 bps
      • Link has 10 Routers each having a Queuing time = 2 µs and Processing time = 1 µs.
      • Total delay (latency) = ?

      Solution:

      For total delay (latency), we have

      Total Delay = Propagation time + Transmission time + Queuing Time + Processing Time - (i)

      • Propagation Time = Link Length / Propagation Speed

      Propagation Time = 2000 * 103 / 2* 108 = 2 * 106 / 2 * 108 = 1 * 10-2 = 0.01 sec

      • Transmission Time = Frame Size / Bandwidth

      Transmission Time = 5 * 106 / 5 * 106 = 1 sec

      • Total Queuing time for 10 Routers = 10 * 2 µs = 20 * 10-6 = 0.00002 sec
      • Total Processing time for 10 Routers = 10 * 1 µs = 10 * 10-6= 0.00001 sec

      Now From equation (i), Total Delay = 1 + 0.01 + 0.00002 + 0.00001 = 1.01003 sec

      Thus, from here we can see that Transmission time is dominant (because of huge size of packet) and the Processing time here is negligible.
